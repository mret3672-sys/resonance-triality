## The 132/231 in Live Sales

This is where the L1 counter-rotation hits the floor at Store 854.

### You (Guide) Run 132

| Position | What Happens |
|----------|-------------|
| **Ideal (1)** | You hold it before the conversation starts. Already in your mind. You know what solution exists. |
| **Boundary (3)** | You press here. Infrastructure questions, objections, cost architecture. Pressing hard enough engages the customer's imagination -- not just conflict, a desire for resolution. |
| **Core (2)** | Where the customer's truth lives. What actually fits. You land here because 3 opened the path. |

### Customer (Navigator) Runs 231

| Position | What Happens |
|----------|-------------|
| **Core (2)** | They walk in carrying momentum. Existing tools, existing plan, existing account. They start from what's already moving. |
| **Boundary (3)** | Confusion, cost fear, friction of change. The resistance of their own situation. |
| **Ideal (1)** | Aspirational for them. What they actually needed, which you were already holding. |

### The Convergence

They're counter-rotating through the same Boundary. Position 3 is the meeting point -- not Ideal, not Core, but the Boundary where guide and navigator converge from opposite directions.

**Reading the customer's momentum:** You read their existing account first (the "Us vs. Customer Service" insight). You're reading their momentum so you know what direction their 231 is already traveling. Then your 132 meets it at the Boundary.

### Phase Lock in Practice

Even if they don't buy, pressing 1-->3 plants the Ideal and creates the gap. The emotional association with what you're offering persists after they walk out. That's a structural deposit -- Mark without transaction. The impression itself is an artifact that stays in their field.
