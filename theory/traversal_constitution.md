## Step 2 — Inverse (Absolute)

**Stand at the epicenter.**

- Take the absolute stance: become the interior of the whole
- Do not observe from outside — inhabit the center

- Determine what operations become valid from this stance

## Step 3 — Margin123

**Count the arithmetic enclosure.**

Margin is **min–span–max**:

```
M = (m_min, Δ, m_max)
```

Where:
- **1 = m_min** — the lower bound
- **2 = Δ** — the counted variation / span between bounds
- **3 = m_max** — the upper bound

### What Margin Does

Margin answers:
- What is the minimum?
- What is the maximum?
- How much variation is enclosed between them?

**Margin is the arithmetic window.** Addition, subtraction, comparison of span — these belong here.

### What Margin Does NOT Do

Margin does not handle:
- Orientation (that is Face)
- Lateral spread (that is Breadth)
- Containment / nesting (that is Depth)
- Multiplication, division, ratio (those belong to the operational axes)

---

## Step 4 — Operate (Face / Breadth / Depth)

**Apply transformations through three operational axes.**

These are not arithmetic. They are the spaces where structure is worked.

### Face

Face is **orientation**.

- From what side?
- Under what stance?
- With what declared leading edge?

Operations: rotate, invert, oppose, declare side.

### Breadth

Breadth is **spread**.

- How wide is the variation?
- How distributed is the whole?
- How much lateral difference is being held?

Operations: widen, narrow, distribute, concentrate.

### Depth

Depth is **containment**.

- How deeply nested is this state?
- What contains it?
- What finer structure does it contain?

Operations: descend, embed, recur, partition inward.

### The Split

| Layer | Domain | Handles |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **Margin** | Arithmetic | Count, delta, min-max span |
| **Face** | Orientation | Rotation, inversion, side |
| **Breadth** | Scale | Distribution, lateral spread |
| **Depth** | Containment | Nesting, recursion, chambering |

This is the correction that untangles everything: **Margin gives the arithmetic window. Face/Breadth/Depth give the transformation space.**
