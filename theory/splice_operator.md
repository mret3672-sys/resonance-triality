### The Strong Claim

The universal candidate is not "golden spiral" as a decorative object. The universal candidate is **ratio-stable self-similar transformation under rotational scaling**.

The golden spiral is a privileged special case if its growth parameter remains invariant across the tested domain.

### The Operator and the Decay Law

The operator chooses how you rotate the spiral:
- Handedness can flip
- Orientation can pivot
- Phase can shift
- Starting radius can move

But the decay/growth law is identity:

```
r = a * e^(b * theta)
```

- **a** sets initial scale
- **theta** is angular progress
- **b** sets the spiral's tightness (radial growth/decay per turn)
- The operator can mess with sign of theta, rotation offset, inversion, frame choice
- But if **b** is fixed, the spiral remains in the same functional family

**Angle is negotiable. Decay law is identity.**
